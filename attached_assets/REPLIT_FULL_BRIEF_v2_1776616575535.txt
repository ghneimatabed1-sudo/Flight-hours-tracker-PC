RJAF FLIGHT HOURS TRACKER
COMPLETE LOGIC & CALCULATION GUIDE FOR DEVELOPERS
================================================================
Written for: Replit development team
Purpose: Full understanding of how flight hours are tracked,
         calculated, and displayed. Read every word.

BEFORE YOU START — UNDERSTAND THE CONTEXT
------------------------------------------
This application is used by the Royal Jordanian Air Force to track
pilot flight hours. It is not a generic app. Every rule in this
document exists because of real military aviation standards.

Getting the calculations wrong is not just a bug — it means a pilot
could appear current when they are not, or their hours could be wrong
in official records. Treat every rule here as non-negotiable.

There are two apps:
  1. PC App  — Used by the Ops Officer to enter flight data.
               Also used by commanders to monitor pilots.
  2. Mobile App — Used by individual pilots to view their own hours.
                  READ ONLY. Pilots cannot enter or change anything.

Both apps connect to the same Supabase (PostgreSQL) database.
The PC writes the data. The mobile reads it.


================================================================
PART 1 — THE THREE FLIGHT CONDITIONS
================================================================

This is the most important concept in the entire system.
Every single flight belongs to ONE of three conditions:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  CONDITION 1: DAY                                       │
  │  A flight conducted during daylight hours.              │
  │  No special equipment. Normal visibility.               │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  CONDITION 2: NIGHT                                     │
  │  A flight conducted at night WITHOUT NVG goggles.       │
  │  The pilot is flying in the dark with no special optics.│
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  CONDITION 3: NVG (Night Vision Goggles)                │
  │  A flight conducted at night WITH NVG goggles.          │
  │  The pilot is wearing special night vision equipment.   │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHY DOES THIS MATTER SO MUCH?
------------------------------
Because the hours from each condition are stored and reported
COMPLETELY SEPARATELY. They never mix. They never add together.

  • Day hours go into the DAY total. Only.
  • Night hours go into the NIGHT total. Only.
  • NVG hours go into the NVG total. Only.

⚠️  THE MOST CRITICAL RULE IN THE WHOLE SYSTEM:
    NVG IS NOT NIGHT. NIGHT IS NOT NVG.
    THEY ARE SEPARATE CATEGORIES.
    AN NVG FLIGHT NEVER ADDS TO THE NIGHT BUCKET.
    A NIGHT FLIGHT NEVER ADDS TO THE NVG BUCKET.
    NEVER MERGE THEM. NEVER SUM THEM TOGETHER.

Example to make this 100% clear:
  A pilot has:
    - 150 hours Day
    - 30 hours Night (flew at night without goggles)
    - 80 hours NVG (flew at night with goggles)

  These are THREE separate numbers. They are all correct.
  The Night total is 30. The NVG total is 80.
  Do NOT add them to get "110 Night hours." That is wrong.
  Report them separately, always.


================================================================
PART 2 — WITHIN EACH CONDITION: SEAT STATUS
================================================================

Now that you understand the three conditions, here is the
next layer: within each condition, the hours are further
separated by the pilot's seat status on that flight.

Every flight has TWO pilots: a Pilot and a Co-Pilot.
Each pilot has a STATUS for that flight. The status options are:

  1st Officer — Flying as the First Officer (primary seat)
  2nd Officer — Flying as the Second Officer (secondary seat)
  Dual        — Flying as part of dual instruction
                (an instructor and student both on the controls)

So the complete breakdown of hour fields in the database is:

  DAY condition:
  ┌────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ day1       │ Day hours flown as 1st Officer             │
  │ day2       │ Day hours flown as 2nd Officer             │
  │ dayDual    │ Day hours flown as Dual instruction        │
  └────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  NIGHT condition:
  ┌────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ night1     │ Night hours flown as 1st Officer           │
  │ night2     │ Night hours flown as 2nd Officer           │
  │ nightDual  │ Night hours flown as Dual instruction      │
  └────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  NVG condition:
  ┌────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ nvg1       │ NVG hours flown as 1st Officer             │
  │ nvg2       │ NVG hours flown as 2nd Officer             │
  │ nvgDual    │ NVG hours flown as Dual instruction        │
  │ nvg        │ Total NVG (nvg1 + nvg2 + nvgDual combined) │
  │            │ Kept for backward compatibility            │
  └────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────────┘

So a pilot's full hour profile looks like this:
  Day 1st:    hours flown as Day 1st Officer
  Day 2nd:    hours flown as Day 2nd Officer
  Day Dual:   hours flown as Day Dual instruction
  Night 1st:  hours flown as Night 1st Officer
  Night 2nd:  hours flown as Night 2nd Officer
  Night Dual: hours flown as Night Dual instruction
  NVG 1st:    hours flown as NVG 1st Officer
  NVG 2nd:    hours flown as NVG 2nd Officer
  NVG Dual:   hours flown as NVG Dual instruction
  Sim:        Instrument simulator hours (separate, explained later)
  Captain:    Hours where this pilot was the aircraft captain


================================================================
PART 3 — THE MOST IMPORTANT DESIGN DECISION:
         PILOT AND CO-PILOT STATUS ARE INDEPENDENT
================================================================

This is the second most critical rule. Read carefully.

When the Ops Officer enters a flight, there are TWO crew members:
  • The Pilot
  • The Co-Pilot

EACH of them has their OWN separate status. Completely independent.

  Pilot:    Choose name + Choose status (1st / 2nd / Dual) + Captain? (yes/no)
  Co-Pilot: Choose name + Choose status (1st / 2nd / Dual) + Captain? (yes/no)

WHY is this so important?

Because in real military aviation, many combinations are possible:

  Example A — Normal training:
    Pilot    = Lt. Ahmad    → 1st Officer,  NOT Captain
    Co-Pilot = Maj. Khalid  → 2nd Officer,  IS Captain
    (The major is flying 2nd seat but is the aircraft commander)

  Example B — Dual instruction:
    Pilot    = Capt. Omar   → 1st Officer,  IS Captain (instructor)
    Co-Pilot = Lt. Faris    → Dual,         NOT Captain (student)

  Example C — Check ride:
    Pilot    = Lt. Nasser   → 1st Officer,  NOT Captain
    Co-Pilot = Col. Ibrahim → 2nd Officer,  IS Captain (evaluator)

In every example above, the Pilot and Co-Pilot have DIFFERENT statuses.
The old broken system had ONE status toggle for both pilots combined,
which made it impossible to represent reality correctly.

The CORRECT design: each crew member gets their own independent
status buttons and their own Captain checkbox. No shared toggles.

⚠️  DO NOT make a single "Position" selector that forces
    Pilot=1st and Co-Pilot=2nd automatically.
    That is the old bug. Do not recreate it.


================================================================
PART 4 — FLIGHT DURATION: ONE TIME, TWO PILOTS
================================================================

Every sortie has ONE time field. One number. For example: 1.5 hours.

This is the duration of the entire flight.

BOTH pilots receive this SAME duration credited to their own bucket.

  If the flight is 1.5 hours and condition is DAY:
    Pilot (1st Officer):    gets 1.5 hours in day1
    Co-Pilot (2nd Officer): gets 1.5 hours in day2

  They were BOTH in the aircraft for 1.5 hours.
  The hours are NOT split between them.
  The hours are NOT shared.
  Both pilots independently receive 1.5 hours, each in their own bucket.

This might feel like "double counting" but it is not. These are two
separate pilots. Each one's logbook gets credited for the time they
personally spent in the aircraft. A 1.5-hour flight adds 1.5 hours
to each pilot's personal record — in the appropriate category.


================================================================
PART 5 — CAPTAIN HOURS
================================================================

Captain hours are a running total of hours where a specific pilot
was acting as the Aircraft Commander (Captain).

Captain hours are NOT a separate flight condition.
They are an ADDITIONAL credit on top of the condition hours.

  If Pilot is marked Captain on a 1.5-hour Day flight:
    → Pilot gets 1.5 hours in day1 (their 1st Officer Day hours)
    → Pilot ALSO gets 1.5 hours in their Captain total
    → These are not double-counted in the Grand Total —
      Captain hours are a separate stat, not added to Grand Total

  If Co-Pilot is ALSO marked Captain on the same sortie:
    → Co-Pilot gets 1.5 hours in day2 (their 2nd Officer Day hours)
    → Co-Pilot ALSO gets 1.5 hours in their own Captain total

Both pilots on the same sortie CAN both be marked Captain.
Neither pilot has to be marked Captain.
These are completely independent checkboxes for each seat.

Captain hours appear in:
  • Pilot detail page (CAP total)
  • Monthly report
  • Rankings page
  • The pilot's Grand Total breakdown


================================================================
PART 6 — HOW THE CALCULATION WORKS (STEP BY STEP)
================================================================

When computing a pilot's total hours, the system does the following:

Step 1:
  Collect ALL sorties in the database where this pilot appears
  as either the Pilot OR the Co-Pilot.

Step 2:
  For each of those sorties, look at three things:
    a) The sortie's condition (Day / Night / NVG)
    b) The sortie's time (the flight duration in decimal hours)
    c) Whether this pilot was marked as Captain on that sortie

Step 3:
  Route the time into the correct bucket based on condition ONLY.
  NVG goes to NVG. Night goes to Night. Day goes to Day. Never mixed.

Step 4:
  If the Captain flag is true for this pilot on this sortie,
  also add the time to the Captain total.

Step 5:
  Add up everything, then add the opening balance
  (the hours the pilot had before they were entered into the system).

GRAND TOTAL formula:
  Grand Total = Opening Day + All Day sortie hours
              + Opening Night + All Night sortie hours
              + Opening NVG + All NVG sortie hours
              + All Simulator hours

Monthly Total formula (same logic, but only current month sorties):
  Month Total = Day hours this month
              + Night hours this month
              + NVG hours this month
              + Sim hours this month

PART 7 — INSTRUMENT FLIGHT AND IRT CURRENCY
================================================================

Instrument flight is a separate optional section that can be added
to ANY sortie, regardless of whether it is Day, Night, or NVG.

It is NOT its own condition. Think of it as "additional information
about this flight" rather than a category of hours.

The instrument flight fields are:

  instrumentFlight → True/False flag: was this an instrument flight?
  ifSim  → Hours flown using instruments inside a simulator
  ifAct  → Hours flown using instruments in real flight (under hood)
  ils    → Count of ILS (Instrument Landing System) approaches done
  vor    → Count of VOR approaches done

IMPORTANT: These hours are NOT added to the pilot's Day/Night/NVG totals.
They are tracked in their own column for IRT currency purposes.

The `sim` legacy field in the database stores the ifSim value for
backward compatibility with older reports and the mobile app.

IRT CURRENCY:
  Every pilot needs to keep their Instrument Rating current.
  The IRT currency has its own expiry date stored on the pilot's profile.
  Default validity: 180 days (configurable per squadron).

  UNLIKE Day and Night currencies, the IRT expiry is NOT automatically
  updated when a sortie is saved. The Ops Officer must manually update
  the IRT expiry date after a pilot completes an instrument refresher.


================================================================
PART 8 — THE FIVE CURRENCY TYPES
================================================================

Every pilot has five currencies. Each one is an expiry date.
If the date has passed, the pilot is expired in that category.

  ┌──────────┬────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Currency │ What it tracks                                     │
  ├──────────┼────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ Day      │ Last day flight. Expires if not flown for 60 days. │
  │ Night    │ Last night OR NVG flight. 60 days. (See note below)│
  │ IRT      │ Instrument rating refresher. 180 days. Manual.     │
  │ Medical  │ Medical certificate. 365 days. Manual.             │
  │ Sim      │ Last simulator session. Manual entry.              │
  └──────────┴────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

CRITICAL NOTE ABOUT NIGHT CURRENCY:
  The Night currency expiry covers BOTH Night flights AND NVG flights.
  They share one currency field.
  When a Night sortie is saved → Night expiry auto-updates.
  When an NVG sortie is saved → Night expiry also auto-updates.
  This is per RJAF SOP for the UH-60M. It is intentional.
  Do not separate them into two different currency fields.

AUTO-UPDATE RULE (happens automatically when a sortie is saved):
  • Day sortie saved   → Day currency expiry for BOTH pilots moves forward
                         to: (sortie date + 60 days)
  • Night sortie saved → Night currency expiry for BOTH pilots moves forward
                         to: (sortie date + 60 days)
  • NVG sortie saved   → Night currency expiry for BOTH pilots moves forward
                         to: (sortie date + 60 days)

NEVER MOVE EXPIRY BACKWARDS:
  If a pilot already has a night expiry of 2025-09-15 and you log
  a new night sortie with date 2025-06-01, the new calculated expiry
  would be 2025-07-31 — which is EARLIER than 2025-09-15.
  In this case, KEEP 2025-09-15. The expiry only ever moves forward.

CURRENCY STATUS LEVELS (from most to least urgent):
  expired      → Date has already passed. Pilot is not current.
  critical     → Expires within a very short time (days away).
  expiringSoon → Getting close (weeks away).
  warning      → Approaching (a month or so away).
  current      → Valid with plenty of time remaining.

These statuses drive color indicators on the dashboard, alerts,
and the mobile app. Red = expired/critical. Amber = warning.
Green = current.

HIDDEN CURRENCIES:
  Some pilots may never fly a certain type. For example, a pilot who
  only ever flies NVG missions may never have a Day currency to track.
  The Ops Officer can mark any currency as "N/A" for a specific pilot.
  Hidden currencies show as "N/A" — not as "expired." They do not
  count toward the alert/warning totals.


================================================================
PART 9 — OPENING BALANCES
================================================================

When a pilot joins the system, they already have years of flying
history. All those hours before the app existed must be stored somewhere.

These are called Opening Balances:
  openingDay   → Total Day hours flown before the system was set up
  openingNight → Total Night hours flown before the system was set up
  openingNvg   → Total NVG hours flown before the system was set up

These are entered once when the pilot is first added to the roster.
They are permanent. They are added to all calculated totals.

Grand Total Day = openingDay + sum of all Day sorties in the system
Grand Total Night = openingNight + sum of all Night sorties in the system
Grand Total NVG = openingNvg + sum of all NVG sorties in the system

================================================================

Every sortie is classified by its purpose. This is a label only.
It does NOT change how hours are calculated.
The calculation is always driven by Condition (Day/Night/NVG) and
Status (1st/2nd/Dual), not by the sortie type.

  MTF          Maintenance Test Flight

  TRG DAY      Training (Day)

  TRG NVG      Training (NVG)

PART 11 — THE ADD SORTIE FORM (FULL FIELD LIST)
================================================================

This is the form the Ops Officer uses to log a flight.
Here is every field, what it means, and what it does:

FLIGHT INFORMATION:
  Date         → The date the flight happened. Format: YYYY-MM-DD.
  A/C Type     → Aircraft type. Options: UH-60M, UH-60L, UH-60AIL, AS332.
  A/C Number   → The aircraft tail number. Free text (e.g. "832").
  Sortie Type  → Select from the list above. If "Other…" is selected,
                 a text box appears for free entry.
  Time (hrs)   → Flight duration in decimal hours. E.g. 1.5 for 1h30m.
                 This is ONE number for the whole flight. BOTH pilots
                 receive this duration in their own respective buckets.
  MSN/Duty     → Mission name or duty description. Optional. Free text.

FLIGHT CONDITION (three separate buttons, pick one):
  [ DAY ]   [ NIGHT ]   [ NVG ]
  These are mutually exclusive. Only one can be selected.
  This determines which hour bucket ALL the hours go into.

PILOT SECTION (for the first crew member):
  Name         → Dropdown of all pilots on the squadron roster.
  Status       → Three buttons. Pick one:
                   [1st Officer]  [2nd Officer]  [Dual]
                 This is INDEPENDENT from the Co-Pilot's status.
                 The Ops Officer picks whatever is true for this pilot.
  Captain      → Checkbox: "Captain (CAP)"
                 Tick this if this pilot is the Aircraft Commander
                 for this sortie. Independent from Co-Pilot's checkbox.

CO-PILOT SECTION (for the second crew member — fully independent):
  Name         → Dropdown of all pilots on the squadron roster.
  Status       → Three buttons. Pick one:
                   [1st Officer]  [2nd Officer]  [Dual]
                 COMPLETELY SEPARATE from the Pilot's status.
                 The Ops Officer can set any combination.
  Captain      → Checkbox: "Captain (CAP)"
                 Completely separate from the Pilot's checkbox.

INSTRUMENT FLIGHT SECTION (optional, shown when checkbox is ticked):
  SIM hours    → Time spent under instrument conditions in a simulator.
  Actual hrs   → Time spent under instrument conditions in real flight.
  ILS          → Number of ILS approaches completed this sortie.
  VOR          → Number of VOR approaches completed this sortie.
  These do NOT add to Day/Night/NVG totals. Tracked separately for IRT.

REMARKS (optional):
  Free text. Anything the Ops Officer wants to note:
  weather conditions, aborted sortie, maintenance issue, etc.


================================================================
PART 12 — WHAT HAPPENS WHEN A SORTIE IS SAVED
================================================================

When the Ops Officer clicks "ADD SORTIE", the following happens
in this exact order:

STEP 1 — Validation:
  • Time must be greater than zero.
  • Pilot and Co-Pilot cannot be the same person.
  • Both crew sections must have a pilot selected.

STEP 2 — Hour bucket calculation:
  For the Pilot:
    Take the flight time and route it into the correct bucket.
    The bucket is determined by CONDITION + this pilot's STATUS.
    Examples:
      Condition=Day,   Status=1st → goes into day1
      Condition=Night, Status=2nd → goes into night2
      Condition=NVG,   Status=Dual → goes into nvgDual

  For the Co-Pilot:
    Same process, but using the CO-PILOT'S OWN status.
    Completely independent calculation.
    Examples:
      Condition=Day,   Status=2nd → goes into day2
      Condition=NVG,   Status=1st → goes into nvg1

  For Captain hours:
    If Pilot's Captain checkbox is ticked → add time to pilotIsCaptain
    If Co-Pilot's Captain checkbox is ticked → add time to coPilotIsCaptain
    These are independent flags stored on the sortie record.

STEP 3 — Write to database:
  The sortie record is written to Supabase with all fields populated.
  Including: all bucket values, both pilot IDs, both status flags,
  both captain flags, condition, time, instrument data, remarks.

STEP 4 — Currency auto-update:
  For BOTH the Pilot AND the Co-Pilot, the system checks their
  currency expiry dates and bumps them forward:
    Day sortie   → bumps the Day expiry date forward
    Night sortie → bumps the Night expiry date forward
    NVG sortie   → bumps the Night expiry date forward
                   (because Night and NVG share one currency)
  Rule: only move forward. Never backwards.

STEP 5 — UI refresh:
  React Query cache is invalidated.
  Every open page in the app that shows pilot data updates immediately.
  No page refresh needed. It happens automatically.

STEP 6 — Mobile sync:
  The mobile app polls for new data every 15 seconds.
  Within 15 seconds of the sortie being saved, the pilot can see
  their updated hours on their phone.


================================================================
PART 13 — THE MOBILE APP IN DETAIL
================================================================

The mobile app is the pilot's personal logbook on their phone.
It is strictly READ ONLY. Pilots cannot add or change anything.

FIRST TIME SETUP (linking the phone):
  1. Ops Officer opens the pilot's profile in the PC app.
  2. Ops Officer clicks "Issue Link Code."
  3. A 6-digit number code is generated and shown ONCE.
  4. Ops Officer gives this code to the pilot (by phone, radio, etc.)
  5. Pilot opens the mobile app for the first time.
  6. Pilot enters their Military Number + the 6-digit code.
  7. App connects to the server, validates the code, links the device.
  8. The code is immediately marked as used — it cannot be reused.
  9. The code expires in 7 days if not used.
  10. If a pilot gets a new phone or reinstalls, the Ops Officer must
      issue a new code.

WHAT THE PILOT SEES:
  Home screen:
    • Their name, rank, squadron
    • Grand total hours (Day / Night / NVG / Total)
    • This month's hours breakdown
    • Half-year breakdown: H1 (Jan-Jun) and H2 (Jul-Dec)

  Currency tab:
    • Day currency: expiry date + status color
    • Night currency: expiry date + status color
    • IRT currency: expiry date + status color
    • Medical: expiry date + status color
    • Simulator: last session date + status color

  Log tab:
    • All sorties this pilot has flown (both as Pilot and Co-Pilot)
    • Sorted by date, newest first
    • Each entry shows: date, aircraft, sortie type, condition, hours

  Settings tab:
    • Language preference
    • Notifications for expiring currencies
    • Change PIN
    • Sign out

OFFLINE MODE:
  The app saves a snapshot of all data to encrypted storage on the device.
  If the pilot has no internet, they still see their last synced data.
  The screen shows when the data was last updated.
  Pull-to-refresh manually forces a new sync when online.

PIN LOCK:
  Optional. The pilot can set a 4-digit PIN for privacy.
  After entering the PIN once, the app trusts the device and
  auto-unlocks on future opens. The PIN is only required again
  after signing out or a password change.
  If the pilot forgets their PIN, they must re-pair with a new
  6-digit code from the Ops Officer.


================================================================
PART 14 — WHAT THE COMMANDER SEES (PC - READ ONLY VIEW)
================================================================

Squadron commanders and HQ commanders use the PC app but in
a read-only mode. They CANNOT add sorties or edit pilots.

What they can see:
  • Cross-squadron pilot tables (HQ sees all squadrons)
  • Squadron commander sees their own squadron only
  • Pilot hour totals per category
  • Currency status for every pilot (color coded)
  • Alerts: who is expired, who is expiring soon
  • Simulator records
  • Flight records

The data they see is live from the same Supabase database.
When an Ops Officer logs a sortie, the commander's screen
updates automatically.


================================================================
PART 15 — THE 7 RULES THAT MUST NEVER BE BROKEN
================================================================

These are absolute. Non-negotiable. Do not look for exceptions.

RULE 1:
  NVG hours NEVER go into the Night bucket.
  Night hours NEVER go into the NVG bucket.
  They are permanently, always, completely separate.

RULE 2:
  The Pilot and Co-Pilot status are ALWAYS independent.
  Never use a single toggle that sets both at once.
  The Ops Officer sets each seat separately every time.

RULE 3:
  Both pilots ALWAYS receive the full sortie duration.
  Do not split the hours. Do not share them.
  If the flight is 2.0 hours, both pilots each get 2.0 hours
  in their respective bucket.

RULE 4:
  Currency expiry dates NEVER move backwards.
  If the new calculated date is earlier than what is already stored,
  keep the stored date. Always take the later date.

RULE 5:
  When the system is connected to the live database (Supabase),
  an empty result means there is no data.
  NEVER substitute demo data or mock data into official views
  when the system is live. Empty is correct. Demo data is wrong.

RULE 6:
  A pilot's hours only update based on sorties they were personally
  on (as Pilot or Co-Pilot). A sortie they were not part of
  must never affect their totals in any way.

RULE 7:
  Instrument flight hours (ifSim, ifAct) are NEVER added to the
  Day, Night, or NVG totals. They are a completely separate track
  for IRT (Instrument Rating) currency purposes only.


================================================================
SUMMARY — THE CORE CONCEPT IN SIMPLE WORDS
================================================================

An Ops Officer enters a flight. The flight has:
  • A duration (e.g. 1.5 hours)
  • A condition (Day, Night, or NVG — never mixed)
  • Two crew members, each with:
      - Their own status (1st Officer, 2nd Officer, or Dual)
      - Their own Captain flag (yes or no)

The system takes that 1.5 hours and routes it:
  → Into the Pilot's bucket (based on their status + condition)
  → Into the Co-Pilot's bucket (based on THEIR status + condition)
  → Into Captain totals if either flag is ticked

Both pilots' currency expiry dates advance automatically.

The mobile app shows each pilot their own data only, within 15 seconds.

That is the entire system. Everything else is presentation and security.
